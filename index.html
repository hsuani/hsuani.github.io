Why this candidate

Five things that separate me from a generic ML / TPM candidate for an NVIDIA Metropolis (Manufacturing) role:

1. ISP → AOI bridge, with measured numbers

Two years tuning sensor-level ISP modules at Qualcomm (noise, exposure, alignment, IQ validation across mobile + PC platforms). I'm now applying that same physical model to industrial defect detection — the unique angle is that image quality is upstream of every downstream AOI metric, and it shows in instrumented results, not slides.

Trained YOLOv8s-seg on MVTec AD — mAP@50 0.75 (mask) on metal_nut. TensorRT INT8 5.22× speedup on Tesla T4, 1.13× further on L4. DeepStream 9.0 multi-stream pipeline at 4 streams × 116 fps = 465 img/s aggregate, 3.8× over the typical 30 fps/stream SLA.

2. Pre-registered hypotheses, then measured reality

Before running the ISP-aware robustness study, I committed ADR-0007 with four explicit predictions, then published the verdicts after the 13-cell eval matrix ran. Engineering rigor most ML candidates skip: treat your own predictions like you'd treat a vendor's claims, and report the misses just as clearly as the hits.

Predicted ranking: NOISE > EXPOSURE > ALIGNMENT. Real ranking: NOISE >>> ALIGNMENT > EXPOSURE. H1 partially wrong (magnitude wildly underestimated, exposure / alignment swapped), H3 correct within tolerance, H2 deferred to L4 cross-precision rerun, H4 inconclusive. Gap analysis written up front, not after the fact.

3. Diagnosed a 95% deployment-robustness gap nobody benchmarks

Lab-clean MVTec mAP 0.75 collapses to 0.03 (-95%) under production-line normal sensor noise (SNR 30-40 dB — an LED 1-6 months aged plus minor camera drift). The training pipeline had zero noise augmentation; any real-world deployment is fully OOD. Mitigation reuses the same Poisson + Gaussian noise model that produced the INT8 calibration cache — calibration physics and training augmentation become one artifact instead of two disconnected workflows.

Per-defect breakdown (52 sub-evals) surfaced three non-obvious behaviors: flip is alignment-invariant (rotation-equivariant features learned for the orientation-anomaly class), color baseline is the aggregate bottleneck (0.396) but slightly improves under exposure gain (tonal-contrast cue), and scratch + noise is the most fragile pairing (high-frequency cue drowns first).

4. Full NV-stack hands-on, not single-layer

Worked through every stage: PyTorch fine-tune (M5 MPS) → ONNX export with dynamic batch → TensorRT engine builds with custom IInt8EntropyCalibrator2 → DeepStream 9.0 pipeline (skill-orchestrated via NVIDIA's deepstream-byovm, custom YOLOv8-seg parser, 4-stream batched inference at 465 img/s aggregate on a GCP L4). Not "I have certificates"; it runs end-to-end, the engines, calibration cache, PDF benchmark report, ADR log, sprint-narrative companion, and graphviz architecture diagram are all committed.

5. TPM record, not just an engineer

AOI Defect Detection — TensorRT + DeepStream MVP Current · 2-week sprint

NVIDIA stack · TensorRT · ONNX · YOLOv8-seg · industrial AOI · MVTec AD

End-to-end AOI defect detection pipeline targeting NVIDIA Metropolis (Manufacturing): PyTorch → ONNX → TensorRT (FP32 / FP16 / INT8) → DeepStream. Trained YOLOv8s-seg on MVTec AD; cross-class study (transistor / cable / metal_nut) shows supervised seg ceiling depends more on defect-type composition (surface vs positional) than on sample count — empirically motivating anomaly-detection paradigm for positional-anomaly-dominant cases.

  • Model: YOLOv8s-seg fine-tuned on MVTec metal_nut → mAP@50 0.75 (mask); 3-class iteration study showed supervised-seg ceiling depends on defect-type composition (surface vs positional) more than sample count.
  • TensorRT optimization (Tesla T4, batch=1, imgsz=640): FP16 3.13× speedup (10.91 → 3.49 ms), INT8 5.22× (10.91 → 2.09 ms, 478 qps) over FP32. L4 INT8 1.13× further (1.78 ms, 540 qps).
  • INT8 calibration: implemented IInt8EntropyCalibrator2 with 335 MVTec metal_nut images; functional drift FP16 < 0.001%, INT8 8.6%. Calibration cache portable across GPU tiers (T4 → L4) saved ~5-15 min recalibration per target.
  • DeepStream 9.0 multi-stream pipeline (GCP L4): 4 streams × 116 fps = 465 img/s aggregate, 3.8× the typical 30 fps/stream SLA. Skill-orchestrated via NVIDIA's deepstream-byovm, det-only path per ADR-0006. KITTI dump confirms parser correctness (9876 metal_nut detections in a 4-stream window).
  • ISP-aware robustness study (D8-D9): 13-cell mAP@50 matrix (3 perturbations × 3 severities + combined-apply), per-defect breakdown over 52 sub-evals. 4 hypotheses pre-registered in ADR-0007 before any eval; verdicts published transparently.
  • 95% mAP collapse diagnosis: production-line normal sensor noise (SNR 30-40 dB) drops mAP 0.75 → 0.03. Training had zero noise augmentation; mitigation reuses the calibration-set Poisson + Gaussian noise model as a training transform — one physical noise model bridges quantization design with deployment robustness.
  • Per-defect insight (NV-relevant): flip is alignment-invariant (rotation-equivariant features learned), color baseline (0.396) is the aggregate-mAP bottleneck but slightly improves under exposure gain, scratch + noise is the most fragile pairing.
  • Outstanding work, transparently scoped: cross-precision FP32 / FP16 / INT8 robustness matrix on L4 (verifies INT8-noise compound hypothesis), aggregated heatmap PNG, noise-augmented retrain to convert proposed mitigation into demonstrated. Tracked in notes/day-09.md.
